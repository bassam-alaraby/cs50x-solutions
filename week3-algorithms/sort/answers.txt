sort1 uses: Bubble sort

How do you know?: When the input was already sorted, sort1 was faster,
but it was slower when the input was not sorted.
Bubble sort has Big O of O(n^2) and Big Omega of O(n).

sort2 uses: Merge sort

How do you know?: sort2 was very fast for both sorted and unsorted input.
Merge sort has Big O of O(n log n) and Big Omega of O(n log n).

sort3 uses: Selection sort

How do you know?: sort3 took almost the same time for sorted and unsorted input, and it was slower.
Selection sort has Big O of O(n^2) and Big Omega of O(n^2).
